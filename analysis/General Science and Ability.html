  • Inclusion of Health and Nutrition Topics:

c. Environmental Science

  • Topics: Pollution types (water, air, acid rain), climate change, natural disasters (avalanches, earthquakes, tsunamis), and sustainable practices.
  • Importance: Critical for addressing current global challenges and promoting environmental stewardship.

  • Topics: Fiber optics, artificial intelligence, GPS, computer memory, networking vs. internet, and remote sensing.
  • Importance: Vital for understanding modern technological applications and their impacts on society.

e. Earth and Space Sciences

  • Topics: Astronomical phenomena (eclipses, black holes), earth movements (rotation, revolution), geological formations (volcanoes, tectonic plates), and cosmic theories (origin of the universe).

  • Repeated Appearances: Definitions, classifications, advantages, disadvantages, and specific examples like wind energy, solar energy, geothermal energy, and biofuels.
  • Importance: Fundamental for understanding sustainable practices and environmental conservation.

g. Mathematical Reasoning

  • Topics: Arithmetic operations, probability, statistics, geometry, and algebra within scientific contexts.
  • Importance: Enhances problem-solving skills and the ability to apply mathematical concepts to scientific questions.
